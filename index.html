Weather Outlook

October and November Forecasts

For October 2025, the forecast indicates 245 HDDs. There is no definitive signal from the Pacific, with ENSO conditions remaining neutral. The tropical season is expected to remain active throughout October. Preferred analogs suggest near-normal conditions from the Plains to the Southeast, while warmer-than-average temperatures are likely across the North and interior West. The tropics will remain a significant wild card.

The November 2025 forecast predicts 510 HDDs. The main idea is that the eastern U.S. will experience mild conditions, while cold air is expected to develop in western Canada. The Eastern U.S. is likely to remain warmer than normal, though variations could allow for a burst of cold air to move eastward before the month ends.

NOAA Winter Outlook & ENSO Forecast

The upcoming winter is not expected to have a strong ENSO signal for the second consecutive season, fluctuating between ENSO neutral and a weak La Niña at times. This weak signal suggests that variability will be a common theme throughout the winter, similar to last year.

The NOAA winter forecast aligns with a typical La Niña weather pattern: colder and stormier in the Northwest, with better chances for warmer-than-normal temperatures in the South and East. With no dominant ENSO signal, other indices like the NAO and AO could be more influential, potentially leading to multiple, strong cold shots across the South and East this winter.

LNG Export Capacity & U.S. Energy Exports

Currently, over 17 Bcf/d of LNG export capacity is in service, with an additional 2 Bcf/d in the commissioning phase. In the previous year, U.S. oil, gas, and coal exports totaled approximately $200 Billion according to UN Comtrade data.

Natural Gas Storage

The EIA reported a solid injection of 55 Bcf last week, marking the second largest injection of the summer. Current storage stands at 3,272 Bcf, which is 73 Bcf (-2.2%) less than one year ago, but 173 Bcf (5.5%) above the 5-year average.

The EIA’s latest Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO) revised the end of October storage prediction to 3,912 Bcf. Assuming a 10-year normal winter, storage is projected to drop to 1,852 Bcf by the end of March 2026.

Since June 1st, Lower 48 power burns averaged close to 43.4 Bcf/d, compared to 45.8 Bcf/d last year. Higher spot gas prices have contributed to an increase in coal generation.

Storage is currently just under 3.3 Tcf and is on track to be effectively full by the end of October. As power burns begin their seasonal decline in the latter half of the month, there is a potential for producers to slow production if storage approaches 3.9 Tcf earlier than anticipated.

Russia-China Natural Gas Pipeline

Russia and China recently signed a legally binding memorandum for the Power of Siberia 2 pipeline, which will run from western Siberia to China. This pipeline is projected to flow 50 Bcm/yr (~4.8 Bcf/d) of natural gas.

The existing Power of Siberia 1 pipeline, with a current capacity of 38 Bcm/yr, will see an increase of 6 Bcm. This expansion means that the total export capacity from Russia to China will rise to 58 Bcm or 5.6 Bcf/day.

The revival of the Power of Siberia 2 pipeline places China in a strategic position, as Russia seeks to replace its gas sales to Europe, which aims to end Russian purchases by December 2027. China, however, is balancing its dependence on Russian supply with its need to replace U.S. LNG imports, which ceased in March due to reciprocal tariffs.

Offshore Wind Industry Challenges

The offshore wind industry in the U.S. is facing significant headwinds due to recent governmental actions, creating capacity and energy market uncertainty in northeast markets. The loss of new generation from canceled renewable projects may lead to more opportunities for fossil fuel infrastructure, including natural gas pipelines.

Recent Developments:

  • April 16: Bureau of Energy Oceanic Management (BOEM) issued a work-stop order on Empire Wind in New York.
  • July 29: Interior Secretary Doug Burgum rescinded designated offshore “wind energy areas” intended for speculative wind development.
  • July 30: 12 offshore wind grants and project selections lost $679M of funding from the Department of Transportation.
  • August 22: Interior Department ordered all activities halted for the Revolution Wind project and intends to revoke the Maryland Offshore Wind project’s construction and operations plan (COP).
  • August 29: Interior Department intends to reconsider Southcoast Wind’s COP approval.
  • September 3: BOEM to reconsider New England Wind 1 & 2’s COP.

PJM Capacity Auction Results

PJM’s capacity auction results continue to set records, as rapidly increasing demand catches the market flatfooted after years of sufficient resources and low clearing prices. The supply side is impaired by supply chain issues, expiring tax incentives, and long interconnection queue delays.

Auction Result Drivers:

  • 5,446 MW increase in capacity requirement.
  • Reserve margin increased from 17.8% to 19.1%.
  • Supply offered decreased by 500 MW from the previous auction.
  • Energy efficiency resources were not eligible to participate.
  • Demand Response levels were similar.

The price would have been $388.57/MW-day if a price cap had not been in place.
